FAQ

What time should I arrive?

Our ceremony starts at 12pm sharp, so please arrive promptly at 11:30am to ensure you are seated and ready for the main event! Please give yourselves plenty of time to reach the Church — it is a Saturday, the King's Birthday & World Cup, so there may be some extra traffic. But once at the Church, you won't have to travel again for the reception!

Are the Church & Grounds secure?

For those parking on site (with priority given to family and the bridal party), your car will be safe and within view. The main gates to the Church grounds will remain open throughout the day, as it is public, though usually quiet. The gated garden where our picnic reception will take place has smaller gates, which will likely remain open so you can leave for the bathrooms or take a look around. Therefore, parents, please keep a close eye on your children — thank you!

Can I bring a plus one?

Originally, Cam and I planned a smaller wedding, but we quickly realised we know too many cool people and our guest list doubled! As a result, we've reached full capacity and kindly ask that only those personally invited attend. While we wish we could include everyone we know, we've chosen you to celebrate with us, and we're so thrilled to have you there.

What is the dress code?

We're planning a relaxed, summery garden party reception inspired by the many picnics where we fell in love, so picnic blankets will be part of the day! We're praying for sunshine (with a Plan B in place), so we recommend lighter suits and summery dresses. Kindly avoid tan suits and long satin dresses, as our bridal party has beat you to it! Also, no stilettos — we don't want anyone sinking into the grass! There will be shaded areas and some fans provided, but feel free to bring your own, plus sunglasses.

Will there be food for dietary requirements?

We're continuing our picnic-inspired vision with our paid catering, so there will be plenty of food for everyone! Many of our wonderful family and friends are also bringing savoury dishes, and some are baking cakes for later in the afternoon (our lovely family friend Emma is making our wedding cake!). We have made sure to include vegan and gluten-free options and have invited some of our church friends to bring dishes representing their cultures. We're so excited to share all the incredible food!

Will there be plenty of drinks?

There will be a range of cold drinks provided, including water, soft drinks, beers, wine & prosecco! We would like to encourage our guests to drink sensibly and responsibly — we want to make sure everyone is hydrated and enjoying themselves. If anybody has extra coolers, please do contact us, it would be super helpful!

Am I invited to the evening dinner?

We have decided to have a very small, intimate dinner with our closest family & friends to enjoy the end of such a wonderful day. Those who will be attending have been invited separately. We want our picnic reception to be the main celebration, which is why we want everyone to be a part of it!

What happens after the Bride & Groom exit?

The Bride & Groom exit is at 6:30pm (get that arch ready!) and we will be heading to our dinner venue. Post our exit, please do help yourselves to any extra food — takeaway boxes will be provided. Our bridal parties will be helping set down, but for any of you who are willing to hang around and help in any way or take down decorations, we would deeply appreciate it. Our hired furniture is set to be collected at 7pm.
